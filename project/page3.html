Electric Vehicles in The Philippines

MANILA, Philippines — This could be the year of the electric vehicle in developing countries, which would help reduce air pollution and allow countries to end dependence on imported fossil fuels, the Sustainable Mobility Unit at the United Nations Environment Program (UNEP) said. Rob de Jong, head of the Sustainable Mobility Unit at UNEP, said the shift to electric transport has a chance to transform cities around the world, helping them reduce air pollution and rein in planet-warming greenhouse gases. De Jong, who leads a landmark UNEP effort that is helping some 60-plus developing countries speed their transition to electric vehicles (EVs), said there are several benefits of electric vehicles. “They would support the growth of domestic renewable energy sources – like hydro and geothermal – which many countries have in abundance… if vehicles were manufactured locally, they could create a huge amount of green jobs. Finally, they would help cut down the greenhouse gas emissions that are driving climate change,” De Jong said.

The Rise of Electric Vehicles in the Philippines

There is a growing number of Filipinos opting to shift to electric cars for personal use. Government data shows that more than 10,000 EV units were sold in 2023. Of these, about 9,200 were hybrid electric motors or units that combine a gasoline-powered engine and an electric propulsion system. Because of its many advantages, the Philippine government is encouraging the adoption of electric cars. Executive Order 488 (s. 2006) supports EV manufacturing by reducing tariffs on EV components, parts, and accessories, making imports more affordable. In January 2023, the government completely removed tariffs on completely for five years to boost the local electric vehicle market and support the shift to emerging technologies. EV owners also receive tax incentives, priority registration, fee discounts, special license plates, and exemptions from traffic volume reduction schemes. Moreover, the government is pushing for the PUV modernization program, which mandates the transition from gasoline-powered jeeps to electric jeeps. In Metro Manila, e-jeeps and e-trikes are also increasingly becoming more popular. The development of “green cities” is expected to further increase the demand for e-vehicles in public transportation.

Electric Vehicles in Sustainable Development in Philippines

As the Philippines confronts the intertwined challenges of climate change and air pollution, electric vehicles (EVs) have emerged as a beacon of hope. These vehicles present a remarkable opportunity for the nation, not only benefiting individual consumers but also propelling the broader ambition of sustainable development. By strategically incorporating EVs into the national transport framework, the Philippines stands to reap substantial environmental, economic, and social rewards. This article will explore the multifaceted role of electric vehicles in advancing sustainability within the Philippine context, addressing the inherent challenges, and underscoring governmental endeavors designed to foster a greener and more sustainable transportation system for the future.

Understanding Electric Vehicles in Detail

Electric vehicles (EVs) are essentially vehicles powered, either entirely or partially, by electricity. A key distinction from conventional gasoline-powered vehicles is that EVs rely on electricity stored in batteries, which translates to zero tailpipe emissions. This feature makes them a powerful tool in combating air pollution and mitigating global warming, especially in densely populated urban centers. The world is increasingly recognizing electrification as a critical strategy for addressing climate change, driving the surging popularity of EVs. With numerous voices from across industries calling for cleaner transportation alternatives, the transition to electric mobility is gaining momentum, positioning EVs as vital players in the eco-friendly transport revolution. Think of it this way: instead of burning fuel and releasing harmful gases, EVs quietly hum along, powered by clean energy.

The Environmental Advantages are Undeniable

The environmental benefits of electric vehicles are a major attraction. Traditional vehicles, which depend on fossil fuels, are significant contributors to greenhouse gas emissions – the primary drivers of climate change. Electric vehicles, on the other hand, produce no direct emissions. This has a direct impact on improving air quality. In heavily polluted areas like Metro Manila, shifting towards EVs could dramatically lower the levels of pollutants in the air. A recent study published by the World Health Organization (WHO) indicated that reducing air pollution could lead to a significant decrease in respiratory illnesses and improve overall public health. Imagine breathing cleaner air just by switching the type of vehicle we use!
